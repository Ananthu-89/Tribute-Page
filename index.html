Sir Alex Ferguson

The greatest manager in the history of football

Ferguson with the trophies after the treble win in 98-99 season

  • 1941 - Born in Glascow, Scotland

    • Notable moments as a player

  • 1957 - Debuts for Queenspark Rangers at the age of 16
  • 1967 - Signs for Glascow Rangers for a then record transfer fee, scoring 66 goals from 89 games for the Scottish Giants
  • 1974 - Retires as a player

    • Decorated managerial career

    Clubs in Scotland

  • 1974 - First full-time manager role with St.Mirren in Scotland
  • 1977 - St. Mirren wins the first division championship under Ferguson
  • 1978 - Fired by Mirren, he joins Aberdeen as manager
  • 1980 - Under Ferguson, Abderdeen breaks a 15-year run of Celtic-Rangers dominance by winning the Scottish First Division.
  • 1982 - Wins the Scottish Cup with Aberdeen
  • 1983 - Ferguson managed Abderdeen to a Cup Winners Cup, eliminating the likes of Bayern Munich and Real Madrid. They’d also retain their Scottish Cup and,the same year, win the UEFA Super Cup
  • 1984 - Aberdeen wins the league-cup double.
  • 1985 - Ferguson wins the third league title at Aberdeen
  • 1986 - After winning a Scottish and League Cup Ferguson leaves for Manchester United. He also managed Scotland in the World Cup.

    • Manchester United

  • 1986 - Sign as Manchester United manager
  • 1990 - Wins the FA Cup, his first trophy at club
  • 1991 - Wins the European Cup winners cup
  • 1992 - Wins the European Cup winners cup and later the European Super Cup
  • 1993 - Man United wins the first league title in 26 years
  • 1994 - Man United wins the league FA Cup double for the first time
  • 1995 - Ferguson promotes young players like Gary Neville, Phil Neville, David Beckham, Paul Scholes and Nicky Butt famously known as "Fergie's Fledglings" later
  • 1996 - Man United wins the league FA Cup double again despite critics claiming that "United can't win anything with kids"
  • 1997 - Back to back Premier League title wins
  • 1999 - Most famous year in Sir Alex's and Manchester United history as they win the treble - Premier League, FA Cup and the Champions League beating Bayern in the final and also becomes Knighted by the Queen as 'Sir Alex Ferguson'
  • 2000 - United wins the 6th league title in the PL era
  • 2001 - Champions again for the 7th time
  • 2002 - After initially announcing his retirement he extends his contract with the club.
  • 2003 - Fergie wins his 8th PL title with the club
  • 2004 - Despite losing the title to Wenger's Arsenal "invincibles", Manchester United wins the FA Cup, the last in Fergie's career
  • 2006 - United wins the league Cup, first time in 14 years
  • 2007 - United ends the 3 year league trophy drought to win the Premier League title
  • 2008 - Manchester United wins Premier League title and wins a Champions League for the second time under Sir Alex and went on to win the club World Cup
  • 2009 - For the second time Fergie win three league titles in a row and wins the League Cup
  • 2010 - Wins another League Cup
  • 2011 - Wins the 12th League title
  • 2013 - Ferguson wins his 13th league title with Manchester United, bringing his total major trophy haul at United to 28 and announces his retirement from football

“It is incredible how many careers he has touched. Twenty-six seasons is difficult to get your head around. Ten years is an awful long time for a manager to be at a club in football but Sir Alex went on and on. He has a very strong desire to give his best every single day. That is what he does. He puts everything he has in.” - Ruud van Nistelrooy
